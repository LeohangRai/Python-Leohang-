So up to now we've been talking about indefinite loops,
and that's the use of the while keyword that just runs
until some logical condition is false or you hit a break.
Definite loops are finite.
They are going over, say,
all the lines in the file or all the items in a list
or all the characters in a string or something.
And for this, we use the "for" construct.
And it's more predictable
